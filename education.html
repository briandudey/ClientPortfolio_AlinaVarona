EDUCATIONAL MISSION STATEMENT

My philosophical view of education—education as a transformative tool for individuals, communities and countries—continues to inform my personal and professional choices. In addition to my professional experiences working with culturally and economically diverse populations, I have a theoretical background in student development, equity, and social justice in higher education which serves to contextualize my experiences as an educator.

 

I believe in treating students and my colleagues respectfully by remaining professional, accessible, informed, and by providing timely responses. By exposing students to opportunities they were previously unaware of, we can serve as a catalyst to student engagement and excellence.

Working with students and families from under-resourced communities affirms my belief in the value of education. I see a deep need for students and families unfamiliar with the intricacies of higher education to find advocates who can help them navigate this landscape. In the future, I intend to continue my work with underrepresented and under-served groups in education by advocating for those students typically marginalized by the academic process including: students of color, first-generation students, and students at great risk of academic and workforce disconnection.

FORMAL TRAINING

Post Secondary Administration and Student Affairs, or PASA, is a higher education focused graduate program within USC’s Rossier School of Education. This M.Ed. program equips candidates to enter various fields in higher education including: advising, student affairs, and administration. PASA emphasizes a balance of theoretical and practical knowledge while maintaining a focus on research. I have included an overview of my formal training along with some samples of my work.

EDHP 551: Applied Educational Ethnography

In this course, I studied qualitative research designs and ethnographic research approaches. The class culminated with a self designed qualitative research project.  (Attachment)

EDHP 500: Foundations of Higher Education

I explored contemporary issues in higher, adult, and professional education in the United States through a historical framework with a strong emphasis on implications for policy and practice in higher education. (Attachment)

EDUC 609: Academic Advising in Post Secondary Education

Using historical and theoretical frameworks, this course explored theory, practice, and professional development within the field of academic advising. It aims to equip new advisors to help students achieve academic success while addressing and supporting personal and vocational needs.

EDHP 687: Student Development in Higher Education

With an emphasis on cognitive and developmental theory, our class explored student development and the application of developmental models for program design, interventions, outreach, and research programs.

EDHP 552: Politics of Difference

This course focused on strategies for restructuring institutions of higher education to improve student support and achievement among historically underserved groups.

EDHP 593: Master’s Seminar

This class served as the culminating project for PASA graduate students in their final year. It contained case study projects, a capstone portfolio, exposure to and use of new media, and an analysis of research and literature in post secondary education.

EDHP 657: Management of Student Services in Higher Education

I explored policies and theories for governance and management in higher education including: analysis and applications of current research as well as leadership models.

EDUC 531: Survey of Student Disabilities in Higher Education

This survey course provided an expansive overview of the history of the disability movement, as well as current research on the success of students with disabilities in higher education. It incorporated legal and management issues are also discussed.

EDHP 587: Field Work in Higher, Adult, and Professional Education

Off-Campus Fieldwork allowed me to gain supervised experiences in a variety of offices and settings and pursue special projects in my areas of interest.

EDPT 550: Statistical Inference

This course provided an overview of quantitative research practices.  Application of statistical techniques in education; emphasis on underlying principles and concepts coupled with select inferential techniques using current software, such as Statistical Package for the Social Sciences (SPSS) were included in a final quantitative case study project.  (Link)

EDHP 679: Legal Issues in the Administration of Higher Education

During this course, I analyzed legal issues, Case Law, and Federal and State statutes related to the administration of higher education and its constituents: students, faculty, staff, alumni, and campus communities.

EDHP 563: Student Affairs Work in College

This foundational course provided practical application and exploration of the principles, services, and organizational patterns of student affairs programs and services for two-year, four-year and professional higher education institutions.

EDHP 580: The Community College

This course provided a holistic view of the educational landscape by including an in depth introduction to two-year colleges. It included: the community college movement, history, aims, curriculum, teaching procedures, service populations, current issues, and future implications.

FUTURE GOALS

In the future, I intend to continue my work with institutions and organizations that share my passion for serving students who struggle with access and success in higher education. I hope continue my work on projects and initiatives, which have the potential for greater impact: large scale proposals and projects aimed at systemic change; projects that target the educational outcomes of entire populations. This includes California's current educational equity initiatives, workforce and economic development initiatives, and additional nationwide innovations being implemented in colleges and communities across the country. While my specialization is higher education, I am also interested in the college preparedness process for secondary students, particularly those who live in areas with high rates of poverty, crime, incarceration, and high school incompletion.

My specific focus will be on pathway creation and bridge programming that better connects these student to college and career and closes the rate of disconnection for high school graduates as well as those who do not complete high school.
